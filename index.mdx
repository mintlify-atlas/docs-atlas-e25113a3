Antibody Sequence Landscape is a reproducible research analysis that measures how well protein language model (PLM) embeddings capture species-level structure in VH antibody sequences. It embeds 750 sequences (250 each from human, mouse, and rat) under five conditions spanning two models — ESM2 and AntiBERTy — and three pooling strategies, then quantifies species separation using cosine silhouette with paired bootstrap confidence intervals.

## Key findings

AntiBERTy substantially outperforms ESM2-8M at encoding inter-species structure in VH sequences. For ESM2, switching from the CLS token to full-sequence mean-pooling recovers +0.056 silhouette points — but CDR masking adds nothing on top. AntiBERTy reverses this: framework positions carry inter-species signal under antibody-specific pretraining, and CDR masking slightly hurts performance.
